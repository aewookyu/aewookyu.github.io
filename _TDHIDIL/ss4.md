Ten years after the Battle of Baishi Gorge, Zhao Huanrong was promoted to Deputy Garrison Commander of Guangning. He invited a few friends from the military over to his home for drinks.

The rough-and-tumble men drank boisterously until midnight before finally dispersing.

A few neighboring women came to help, and together with Yisu, they cleaned up the main hall and kitchen. Yisu gave them the leftover meat dishes as a token of thanks.

After seeing them off, Yisu bolted the courtyard gate. As she turned around, she saw Zhao Huanrong emerging from the children's wing.

She walked up to him and said, "You're not drunk?"

Zhao Huanrong smiled and said, "If I didn’t pretend to be drunk, who knows how long they’d have carried on. Sending them off early was better."

"I’ll check if the water is heated. Wash up and go to bed early." Yisu headed to the kitchen. A moment later, she returned to the main room with a basin of water and personally helped Zhao Huanrong wash his feet.

"I can do it myself," Zhao Huanrong said, pulling her to sit beside him. He then asked, "Where’s Xueya?"

Yisu replied, “The little girl was busy all evening too. I told her to rest first.”

“We’re not short on money anymore. We can hire more maids and older women to help with the work. You don’t need to do these chores yourself,” Zhao Huanrong said.

Yisu smiled and said, “Aren’t all the neighbors living like this? There will be plenty of expenses in the future, so let’s save wherever we can.” Her smile faded slightly as she continued, “But there’s something I want to discuss with you.”

“What is it?” Zhao Huanrong asked while washing his feet.

“The auntie from the Hua Family who came to help earlier, you know her, right?”

“Yes, I know her. What about her?”

“Her youngest daughter is seventeen this year. She was supposed to get married this autumn, but her fiancé had an unfortunate accident while hunting in the mountains earlier this year and passed away. Their family has lived in the same alley as us for ten years and holds your character in high regard. I hinted at the idea a little, and they seem interested. They’re willing to let their daughter become your concubine. Now that you’ve been promoted, taking a concubine would also be fitting for your status.” Yisu lowered her eyes and spoke softly.

Zhao Huanrong lifted his feet out of the basin, dried them, and tossed the towel aside. He turned to look at Yisu and said, “Did you take the jokes my friends from the army made seriously? Do you think I’m lacking dignity because I don’t have a concubine, so you’re arranging one for me?”

Yisu quickly explained, “It’s not just that. I’m getting older, and with the children to care for, it’s hard to manage everything perfectly. Having a concubine would make it easier to take care of you.”

Zhao Huanrong reached out and held her hand. “Yisu, I was born of a concubine. No one understands better than I do what kind of life an unvalued concubine and her *shu* children endure. And if a concubine and her children are favored in a household, we both know the kind of consequences that can bring.

"Realistically speaking, I don’t know how to handle the issues of concubines and *shu* children appropriately. Emotionally, from the moment I first met you until now, the only woman I’ve ever wanted is you. So, let’s never bring up the subject of taking a concubine again, all right?”

Yisu looked at him, her eyes filled with emotion.

He wasn’t someone who talked much and rarely said so many words at once unless he was eager to make something clear.

“Alright. I won’t bring it up again. And even if you bring it up, I won’t allow it,” Yisu said.

Zhao Huanrong chuckled and nodded. “Alright.”

He pulled Yisu into his arms and said, “Let’s go ahead and buy two maidservants. In a few days, I’ll have to take leave and return to the capital for **Zufu**’s 80th birthday celebration. The trip there and back will take at least a month and a half, even if I rush. I won’t be here to manage things at home, so you’ll need extra help. I didn’t bring you here from the capital to make you suffer. In the past, we had no choice, but now that I hold an official position, even if we hire a few maids to help, no one will have anything to say.”

"En, I’ll listen to you," Yisu replied.

Zhao Huanrong reached out, gently lifted her chin, and looked into her eyes. "Twelve years have passed in the blink of an eye, and you still look the same as the day I first saw you, completely unchanged."

Yisu blushed and pushed him lightly. "Nonsense. I’m the mother of three children. How could I not have changed?"

"In my eyes, you haven’t. You’re still just as beautiful."

Her face grew redder as she looked at him and said softly, "You too."

The candlelight flickered gently, and the room gradually fell silent, replaced by other sounds.

After a long while, Zhao Huanrong, sweat beading on his forehead, carried the foot basin out of the room. He went to the kitchen, boiled a bucket of water, and brought it back to the bedroom.

In the Jing Guogong Residence, preparations for **Guogong Yé**’s 80th birthday celebration had kept Yin Furen and Xu Nian’an busy for nearly two months, with help from the womenfolk of the household. Finally, a few days before the banquet, everything was more or less in order.

That day happened to coincide with Zhao Huanxi’s day off. Early in the morning, as Xu Nian’an woke and tried to get out of bed, Zhao Huanxi clung to her like an octopus, refusing to let her leave.

“Stop messing around! It’s getting late, let me up,” Xu Nian’an said, struggling breathlessly but failing to break free. She gave him a push.

“What’s the rush? Isn’t everything pretty much taken care of? It’s rare for me to have a day off. Stay in bed with me a little longer,” Zhao Huanxi grumbled.

“Oh, is that all you want, just to sleep in a bit? Then what’s your hand doing?”

Zhao Huanxi leaned close to her ear and whispered something with a grin.

Xu Nian’an drawled, “Well, it’s not like I can’t agree…”

Zhao Huanxi hadn’t expected her to say yes and was just beginning to feel excited when she added, “But only if you get up first and weed the entire courtyard.”

Zhao Huanxi froze in place.

Xu Nian’an burst out laughing.

Snapping out of it, Zhao Huanxi propped himself up and climbed on top of her, tickling her mercilessly. “Oh, so you dare laugh at me? Laugh, will you? Let’s see if you’ll dare to laugh at me again in the future!”

“No, I won’t! I won’t! Hahaha! Stop it!” Xu Nian’an flailed and struggled, laughing so hard she could barely breathe.

Zhao Huanxi pulled the blanket up, covering them both underneath it.

Xu Nian’an was about to grumble a bit more, but before she could, he silenced her with a kiss.

As the two were caught in their tender moment, they were startled by Hui **Ge’er**’s crisp, bright voice calling out from the courtyard: “Tong **Ge’er**, are you coming to breakfast?”

Tong **Ge’er**’s soft, milky voice replied, “Yes.”

“Come recite two poems with me first, then we’ll go.”

“Oh.”

Hui **Ge’er** began reciting, “At three, the lamp burns bright; at five, the rooster crows. This is the time for a man to study and grow.”

Tong **Ge’er** stammered, “At three… lamp… at five… rooster… this is… man…”

Hui **Ge’er** asked him, “Is it hard to remember?”

Tong **Ge’er** nodded earnestly.

“Then let’s switch to another one: The year’s plan begins in spring; the day’s plan begins in the morning.”

Tong **Ge’er**: “...…”

Hearing this, Chun **Jie’er** giggled as she ran over to join them.

Hui **Ge’er** sighed and looked at Tong **Ge’er**. “Let’s switch to a shorter one: Youth does not return; morning comes but once a day.”

Tong **Ge’er** tried again, “Youth does not return; morning… morning…”

Chun **Jie’er** chimed in to help, “Morning comes but once a day.”

Hui **Ge’er** continued teaching, “Make the most of time and strive, for the years wait for no one.”

Tong **Ge’er** repeated softly, while Chun **Jie’er** shouted it out loud: “Make the most of time and strive, for the years wait for no one!”

After they finished, Chun **Jie’er** asked Hui **Ge’er**, “Gege, why are you teaching these poems to him?”  

Hui **Ge’er** replied, “To remind him that as a man, he shouldn’t be sleeping in until the sun is high. Otherwise, when he grows up, he’ll end up pulling weeds for a living.”

Looking at the confused faces of his younger brother and sister, and then glancing at the utterly silent main room not far away, Hui **Ge’er** felt quite pleased with himself. Turning on his heel, he said, “Come on, let’s go have breakfast.”

The three little ones fell silent, while under the covers, Xu Nian’an was laughing so hard she curled up into a ball.

Teased by his ten-year-old son, Zhao Huanxi felt both embarrassed and annoyed. He turned to Xu Nian’an and said, “Did you see that? Hui **Ge’er**’s practically walking all over me, and you just don't care!”

Xu Nian’an’s eyes sparkled like the crescent moon. “What’s there for me to care about? He didn’t say anything wrong, right?”

Zhao Huanxi stared at her in disbelief. “No wonder he’s like this, it’s all because you’ve spoiled him! Since you won’t take care of him, I’ll deal with you instead.” With that, he pounced on her like a hungry tiger.

Xu Nian’an laughed and struggled, saying, “Stop it! Or they’ll end up reciting poetry outside the main room door later, hahaha…”

The couple finally got up and washed, just as the little ones finished their breakfast. Since it was a court holiday and the academy was also on break, the children didn’t have to leave for school.

Still feeling resentful, Zhao Huanxi stood in the courtyard, glaring at Hui **Ge’er** for a moment. Hui **Ge’er** glared back just as boldly until Zhao Huanxi snorted and turned toward the outer study.

Suppressing her laughter, Xu Nian’an took the children and headed to Yin Furen’s courtyard.

Zhao Huanxi had not been in his study for long when Zhao Huanxu arrived.

He had traveled for several years and only returned in the third year after Zhao Huanxi went to Tonglu. He had participated in that year’s autumn imperial examination and the following year’s spring examination. Now, he held a position in the Ministry of Rites.

“Huanxi, there’s something I need to ask of you,” he said with a smile as soon as he entered the room.

“What is it?” Zhao Huanxi asked as he motioned for him to take a seat.

“I have a colleague in the Ministry of Rites who is quite passionate about calligraphy and is an admirer of your writing. He’s been wanting to visit you, but he’s afraid of being too forward. So, he asked me to make an introduction and see when you might have time?” Zhao Huanxu explained.

“Any day off works for me,” Zhao Huanxi replied.

Zhao Huanxu nodded. “I’ll let him know, then.”

After they settled the matter, Zhao Huanxu fell silent for a moment before turning to Zhao Huanxi. “There’s something I’ve been meaning to thank you for, but I haven’t found the right opportunity until now.”

Zhao Huanxu stood up and walked over to Zhao Huanxi. He bowed to him with great respect and, after lifting his head, said, “Huanxi, thank you for avenging my father.”

Zhao Huanxi quickly stood up as well and said, “We’re family, there’s no need for such formalities.”

Zhao Huanxu replied, “I did many foolish things when I was young, and now that you’re not holding it against me, it shows your magnanimity. But I can’t just pretend it never happened. To put it simply, if you ever need anything from me in the future, don’t hesitate to ask. There’s no need to be polite.”

Zhao Huanxi nodded. “Then I won’t be polite. My mother asked me to write the character ‘寿’ (longevity) to hang around the manor. After calculating, I’ll need to write over eight hundred of them. I’ve written three hundred and sixty, so the remaining five hundred, could you take care of those for me?”

Zhao Huanxu couldn’t help but laugh. “If I write all of them, what will you do?”

Zhao Huanxi replied, “I’ll rest.”

Zhao Huanxu responded, “I won’t agree to that. I’ll write two hundred and fifty.”

Zhao Huanxi countered, “I’ll write one hundred, and you’ll write four hundred.”

“That won’t do. At most, I’ll write three hundred, and you write two hundred.”

“Deal.”

Zhao Huanxu left Zhao Huanxi's study and walked toward the backyard, where he happened to see Yu **Jie’er** leading Chun **Jie’er** and the other children as they chased butterflies.

“Yu **Jie’er*&, where’s your mother?” he asked.  

Yu &*Jie’er** pointed toward the direction of Jiaxiang House. “Mother’s in Chun **Jie’er**’s **Zumu** courtyard.”

Zhao Huanxu nodded. “Go tell your mother that we won’t be eating at home for lunch. We’re going to Changfeng Lou to have Dongpo pork.”

Yu **Jie’er**’s eyes brightened with excitement. “Really? Can Chun **Jie’er** and Tong **Ge’er** come too?”

Zhao Huanxu replied, “You’ll need to ask their parents first.”

Chun **Jie’er** spoke up, “**Bofu**, please wait. We’ll go ask them right now!” With that, she grabbed Tong **Ge’er** and ran off.

Yu **Jie’er** also ran ahead, calling back as she went, “Don’t go looking for **Shushu** and **Shenshen** yet, find your Hui **Gege** first. He has the most ideas, and if he takes charge, **Shushu** and &*Shenshen** will definitely agree!”

“Right, right, Yu **Jiejie**, you’re really smart.”

Zhao Huanxu watched as the children laughed and disappeared around the corner of the garden path, their playful voices fading. A warmth filled his heart, and at the same time, he felt even more grateful for the words **Zufu** had spoke to him back then, which had awakened him.

In life, most of one’s suffering arises from desiring things that don’t belong to oneself. But once one learns to let go, they will immediately realize that their entire life feels completely different from before.

On the twentieth day of the Seventh Month, **Guogong Yé** celebrated his eightieth birthday.

On this day, the Jing Guogong Residence was adorned with lanterns and streamers, with carriages and horses crowding the streets. The courtyard was bustling with visitors, as dignitaries and nobles gathered in great numbers. The Emperor also sent Hong Feng to present longevity peaches as a congratulatory gift, along with many other birthday tributes.

The younger generation coming to offer their birthday wishes had to enter Songmao Hall in batches, otherwise, there simply wouldn’t be enough space.

Dressed in a brand-new brocade robe embroidered with longevity symbols, **Guogong Yé** sat in a newly made wooden wheelchair, accompanied by Hui **Ge’er**. Witnessing the prosperity and flourishing of his family, he couldn’t help but be moved to tears.

His life had been full of ups and downs, but in the end, he could still depart with dignity and face his ancestors with pride.

The Zhao Family had not declined under his watch, his legacy would continue.

As night fell, the sounds of music and song gradually faded, the wine cups were set aside, and the guests dispersed.

Alone in his study, **Guogong Yé** sat by the window, gazing at the moonlight outside in quiet contemplation.  

Hui **Ge’er** entered and said, “**Tai Yéye**, all the birthday gifts received today have been cataloged and stored. Xiang **Guanshi** has gone to fetch some sobering soup, I came ahead to inform *you*.”

**Guogong Yé** came back to his senses and turned to look at his great-grandson. “I’m sure it was thanks to your help. Otherwise, Xiang Zhong’s old eyes aren’t as sharp as they used to be, he wouldn’t have finished so quickly.”

“That’s my duty,” Hui **Ge’er** replied steadily as he poured a cup of tea and handed it to **Guogong Yé**.  

Taking the tea, **Guogong Yé** said, “You’re much more outgoing than your father was at your age. When he was your size, he was like a little girl, always hiding behind your **Zumu** and refusing to meet people.”

Hui **Ge’er** said, “I can’t be like him. I don’t want to grow up just to pull weeds.”

**Guogong Yé** early choked on his tea, laughing as he set the cup down. “Your father has already come to complain to me. He said you mock his job for requiring him to pull weeds. Do you really mind that your father is responsible for weeding the palace gardens?”

Hui **Ge’er** lowered his head and rubbed his toe against the ground. “I heard the imperial examinations are incredibly difficult, and Father passed them. But in the end, all he does is lead people to pull weeds in the Palace. I just don’t understand, what was the point of him taking the exams?”

**Guogong Yé** said, “Weeding is just one of your father’s duties, not all of it. He is a man who values his family and dislikes scheming for power, which is why he chose such a relaxed post. This way, he has more time to spend at home with your mother and you siblings. Is that not a good thing?

"When I was young, I fought in Liaodong. Each campaign lasted for years, and even when I returned, I could only stay for a day or two before leaving again. Do you wish for your father to be like me?”

Hui **Ge’er** thought carefully for a moment, then slowly shook his head.

*&Guogong Yé** reached out and pulled him close. “I understand what’s in your heart. When children are young, they always dream that their father is a great hero. Well, let me tell you, your father is a great hero.

"He went to war at eighteen and personally killed the Tiele King. The peace in Liaodong today would not have been possible without him. If you don’t believe me, next time your father takes a bath, sneak a look, you’ll see the scars from the battlefield still on his body.”

This was the first time Hui **Ge’er** had ever heard about his father going to war. He found it difficult to imagine what his father had been like on the battlefield. For once, his expression showed a rare hint of confusion, but he obediently nodded.

“It’s getting late. Go on back now, don’t let your **Zumu** and mother worry,” **Guogong Yé** said, patting his head.

Hui **Ge’er** acknowledged the order, then, like a little adult, reminded him, “**Tai Yéye** have to drink the sobering soup before going to sleep.”

**Guogong Yé** chuckled. “Alright, I’ll listen to Hui **Ge’er**.”

Only then did Hui **Ge’er** give him a proper bow, before turning and heading out.

Not long after, Xiang Zhong entered, carrying a bowl of sobering soup.

**Guogong Yé** drank it, then tilted his head back to gaze at the bright moon outside the window, his expression tinged with melancholy.

Xiang Zhong sat with him in silence for a while before asking, “**Guogong Yé**, are *you* thinking of **Da Laoyé**?”  

**Guogong Yé** nodded. “Any news of him?”  

Xiang Zhong shook his head. “Not yet. But there is news of the two brothers, Huanchao and Huanyang.”

**Guogong Yé**’s expression darkened with displeasure. “Those two unfilial sons! No matter his faults, their father always loved them dearly. To repay that kindness with betrayal—disgraceful! Since we have their whereabouts, see to it that they do not live comfortably.”

Xiang Zhong acknowledged the order.

**Guogong Ye?é** sighed, looking back out the window. “Back then, I was too impulsive, too harshly. I should have left him a way out. Now, at his age, wandering alone in the world… I don’t even know if he is alive or dead.”

Xiang Zhong sighed as well. “Who would have thought that after all these years, **Da Laoyé** has never once come back to seek forgiveness?”

**Guogong Yé** said, “He must know he was wrong, that’s why he has no face to return, to see me, to see **Da Taitai**, or Huanxi and the others. Send more men to search for him. If you find him, buy him a house out there, a few acres of land, and two or three servants so he can spend his remaining years in peace. No matter if I’m still around or not, these words stand.”

Xiang Zhong wiped his tears. “**Laonu** will remember.”

<br>

Hui **Ge'er** returned to Shenxiu Courtyard and found that his parents were not there. When he asked the servants, none of them knew where the two had gone. 

Seeing his younger siblings also searching for their parents, Hui **Ge'er** was speechless. After playing with them for a while, he coaxed them to sleep.

Meanwhile, Zhao Huanxi and Xu Nian'an were nestled together on the swing in Yifang Garden, gently swaying back and forth.

The summer night was tranquil. Unnamed insects chirped in the grass, their calls only adding to the peaceful atmosphere.

“**Sanlang**, maybe we should go back. What if the children can't find us and start crying?” After a long silence, Xu Nian'an suddenly spoke.

Zhao Huanxi turned his head to look at her and asked, “Sober now?”

Xu Nian'an propped her head up and shook it, feeling dizzy. “Not yet,” she replied, then leaned against his shoulder again, grumbling, “It’s all **San Jie**’s fault, always egging me on. And Mother’s bayberry wine was too sweet this year. I drank too much without even realizing it."

"Clearly, you were the one who couldn't resist drinking, yet you're blaming Mother and **San Jie**," Zhao Huanxi said.

Xu Nian'an straightened up in protest. "Hey—"

Zhao Huanxi cupped her flushed cheeks with a smile. "So what if you overindulged? I like it when you do. You're adorable when you're drunk."

"Go away!" Xu Nian'an pushed him away and got off the swing, swaying slightly. "I need to check if Hui **Ge'er**, Chun **Jie'er**, and Tong **Ge'er** are asleep."

"At this hour? Their wet nurse would have already put them to bed. You can barely walk straight, how are you going to get back? Be careful, or you'll fall into the lake. Just sleep here."

Catching up to her from behind, Zhao Huanxi scooped her up in his arms. Just as he was about to carry her inside, a brilliant firework bloomed in the night sky toward the southwest.

"It's so late, who's still setting off fireworks?" Zhao Huanxi wondered aloud.

"Are you silly? That's obviously coming from the Palace!" Xu Nian'an, still tipsy, struggled in his arms. "Put me down! I want to watch the fireworks!"

"Watch from inside." Zhao Huanxi carried her to the room, setting her down on the high table beneath the window. Lifting her chin, he leaned in and kissed her.

"You..." Xu Nian'an had only managed a single word before his eager movements pressed her back against the window. The golden bird hairpin in her hair punctured the paper windowpane.

<br>

Under the same fireworks, Liu Fuyi—now an Noble Consort<sup class="footnote-ref" data-footnote="贵妃(Guifei)-rank in the harem higher than a Consort, usually the highest of all (only second to the Empress"></sup>—stood beneath the eaves of her palace chambers, tilting her slender neck as she gazed at the night sky. The vibrant colors of the fireworks reflected in her bright, crystal-clear eyes.

The Emperor stood beside her, holding her hand.

An hour and a half earlier, the fireworks at Jing Guogong Residence had caught her attention. She had remarked on their beauty, so he had immediately ordered people to leave the Palace overnight to buy fireworks just to put on a display for her.

"**Fu Huang**, **Mu Fei**," the Thirteenth Prince mumbled sleepily as he rubbed his eyes and walked over to them, woken by the sound of fireworks.

"Yi'er, come, watch the fireworks with us," the Emperor said, bending down to lift his son into his arms.

Liu Fuyi frowned. "He's not a child anymore. Put him down—he's too heavy."

The Emperor chuckled. "Not a child? He's only nine. Why wouldn't I carry him? **Fu Huang** is very strong, right, Yi'er?"

Yi’er shouted proudly, "En! **Fu Huang** is the strongest! He can even carry **Mu Fei**, so why wouldn't he be able to carry me?"

The Emperor burst into laughter, but before he could enjoy it for long, he suddenly winced and groaned in pain—Liu Fuyi had given him a sharp pinch.

"Alright, alright, I won’t say any more. Let’s just watch the fireworks," the Emperor quickly conceded.

Yi'er, however, pouted on his father’s behalf. "**Mu Fei** is always bullying **Fu Huang**."

The Emperor hurriedly covered his son's mouth. "Shh, say any more, and **Fu Huang** will have to sleep in the study again tonight."

Liu Fuyi couldn't help but curve her lips into a faint smile before tilting her head back to gaze at the brilliant fireworks lighting up the night sky.

She hadn't entered the Palace by choice. She didn’t even know if she truly loved the man beside her.

But for ten years, he had indulged her without limits, spoiled her, and never let anyone mistreat her.  

Even her own father had never given her such treatment.

Since things are like this, then let's just let it be.
